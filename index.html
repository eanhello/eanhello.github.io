Hello, I'm Ean!

PhD Candidate. Diligent More...

Ean High is an advanced doctoral candidate in the English Department at Northwestern University. Working at the intersections of American literature and religion, his dissertation stages religious historiography and literary reading through the endurance of religious practice rather than its dissolution. Sounding literary representations of the atlantic Quaker community from 1650-1865, his project looks to the practices, repertoires, and religious traditions of the Quaker community as guides to literary-critical insight and scholarly method.

Ean is a 2017 Fellow of the Charlotte W. Newcombe Foundation. His research has also been supported by residencies from the Peabody Essex Museum in Salem, Massachusetts and the Special Collections Library of Haverford College.
